Οὖτις - The Meaning of "Nobody"

Outis (Οὖτις) is an Ancient Greek word and name meaning "nobody" or "no one".

It is famously a pseudonym used by the Greek hero Odysseus in Homer's epic poem, The Odyssey. When trapped in the cave of the Cyclops Polyphemus, Odysseus tells the giant his name is "Nobody." After Odysseus blinds him, the other Cyclopes ask who is harming him, and Polyphemus shouts that

"Nobody is murdering me by craft"
so they do not come to his aid. This clever wordplay, a pun on the Greek word mêtis meaning "cunning intelligence," is a classic example of Odysseus's strategic mind.

The name has since been adopted as a pseudonym by various modern artists and writers to hide their identities, and for characters in fiction, such as in the TV series Prison Break and the game Limbus Company.
